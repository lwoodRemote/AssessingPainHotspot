Assumptions |

Where possible, always speak directly to the patient and do not assume that they cannot communicate for themselves or be successfully triaged due to their dementia diagnosis.



Adapt |

Remain calm and show patience throughout the call; adjusting speed and tone to suit the callers needs. Getting frustrated or abrupt can discourage communication.



Speech |

Slow your rate of speech and avoid giving too many instructions at once. Instead, speak slowly and clearly whilst breaking large pieces of information into smaller chunks.



Personalisation |

In conversation, use the patients name to personalise conversation help to retain focus; regularly checking that there is a mutual understanding of what you are trying to convey.



Large pieces of information |

Break down large pieces of information into smaller chunks; confirming with the patient that there is mutual understanding of what is being communicated.



Engage |

Use speakerphone/loud speaker to engage with the patient and carer/family at the same time. If there is any confusion, then the carer/family member can help communicate this.



Listen |

Allow time for the patient to think and reply, being careful not to interrupt their thinking process but also not leaving unnecessary long pauses.



Repetition |

Rephrase questions as opposed to repeating them and answer questions even if they are repeated.
